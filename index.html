About me

AI researcher and academic leader

Dr Mohammad Al-Azawi is an Associate Professor of Artificial Intelligence and Deputy Dean for Academic Affairs, Research and Innovation at Gulf College. His academic career combines artificial intelligence, computer vision and data science research with institutional leadership in academic affairs, research, innovation, quality enhancement and strategic development.

His research is particularly concerned with applying concepts inspired by human perception to machine vision. A major strand of his work examines symmetry and saliency as computational cues for identifying abnormalities in medical images, particularly brain MRI.

Alongside research, Dr Al-Azawi contributes to academic strategy, programme enhancement, quality and accreditation, institutional effectiveness, research culture, partnerships and innovation. His professional philosophy emphasises evidence-based enhancement and the integration of research, teaching and external engagement.

He holds a PhD in Artificial Intelligence from De Montfort University, a Master's degree in Computer Engineering from the University of Baghdad, and a Bachelor's degree in Electrical Engineering from Al-Mustansiriyah University.

Featured research theme

Symmetry as a signal for brain abnormality identification

My work explores the premise that the human brain is broadly bilaterally symmetrical and that local asymmetry may provide a meaningful computational signal for abnormality detection. This research direction combines image analysis, machine learning and human-inspired visual reasoning.
